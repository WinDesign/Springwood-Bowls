What is U3A

Join us

Joining us is easy

Joining our U3A and then participating in our activities is a simple process. Firstly, you need to become a member and this is achieved by completing and lodging the Membership Application Form; use the download button below to obtain this form.

The only restriction applying is you need to be aged 50 years or older and of course, pay the annual membership fee, currently set at $40.00.

While our U3A is designated as Nepean Blue Mountains this does no limit the membership from applicants outside of the named geographic area. However, membership of another U3A does not entitle to membership of the Nepean Blue Mountains U3A. 

Upon receipt of your membership form and dues the secretary (or delegate) will notify you in writing or by email and a membership card will be issued. That it, all finished, now you can select what courses or activities you are interested in.

Enrollment

Enrollment

Before the commencement of first term and usually in about mid-January our annual enrolment days are held. They are held on two separate venues and days, one usually at the Springwood Sports Club, 81 Macquarie Road, Springwood. The other is held at our main office in the Penrith School of Arts, 3 Castlereagh Street, Penrith. The exact times and days for the annual enrolments is set in November/December and notification will appear in our Newsletters. You can of course contact us for details about our enrolment days.


For perspective students who join us post January enrolment in our courses and activities are still possible. You will need to contact the tutor or class leader to ascertain if there are still vacancies available. Be aware that some of the courses and activities are quite popular and there may be a waiting list. For existing member who miss the enrolment days or wish to select another course the same procedure applies; contact the tutor or class leader.


For further questions about our enrolment procedure, use the Contact Page for details about contacting us.   

Become a tutor - class leader

Become a tutor or classs leader

While providing education courses and activities for member is the core function of our U3A, our volunteer tutors and activity leaders are the backbone of the organisation, without them there would be no U3A. Consequently, we are very mindful to the need to attract new tutors and activity leaders and equally, retain and support our existing ones.

So, if you have a strong interest in a particular subject, familiarisation with an activity or interest that you think others might like share, then perhaps you might consider convening your own course or activity group within our U3A.

While some of tutors and activity leaders have prior teaching experience it is not a prerequisite. In fact, anyone who is interested in sharing their knowledge and expertise with other members and contributing to our activities is encouraged to contact us. You can contact one of our Course Coordinators to discuss your ideas, and to support your proposal download and complete our Tutors Application Form.
